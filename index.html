Research

ToUCH is a collaborative project between Texas State University, Concordia University and Knox College, this is an NSF funded project. We are developing novel teaching materials for undergraduate students on how to program and use Heterogeneous Computing Systems. We are using a combination of Heterogeneous systems for our materials such as the Raspberry Pi and mobile phones based on the ARM big.LITTLE architecture and supercomputing systems with Nvidia cards that support CUDA programming. We are currently working on using our materials to gather more information about their effectiveness.
Dragonfly is a High Performance Computer topology, our project was funded by an NSF grant. Working alongside one other student and our advisor Prof. David Bunde, we developed an algorithm to map tasks onto a dragonfly system. I presented our work at various conferences and at the 2018 Consortium of Computing Science Colleges: MidWest Conference we were awarded 1st place for our work.
